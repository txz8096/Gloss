# Design DNA

## Intent

Help a small team understand what needs attention today in a calm, capable, and direct workspace.

## Principles

- Establish hierarchy with type, spacing, and position before using accent colour.
- Keep the working surface quiet; reserve warmth for meaningful choices and status.
- Keep task evidence close to the decision it supports.

## Anti-principles

- Do not use glossy gradients, decorative charts, or floating cards without a task-related purpose.
- Do not make every item equally prominent.

## Trade-offs

- Dense task views may reduce editorial spacing, but must retain one clear primary action and a scannable task hierarchy.
